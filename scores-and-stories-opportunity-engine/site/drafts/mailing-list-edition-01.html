Part 1: How we handle the mixed age list (the strategy answer)

Our list runs from elementary parents to senior parents, and the instinct to write separate newsletters is the wrong instinct. One send, four laddered sections, clearly labeled by stage, with a one line "jump to your kid" menu at the top. Three reasons this beats segmenting:

First, families have multiple kids. The parent reading for their junior has a sixth grader, and seeing both sections in one email is the moment they realize we think about the whole arc, not just application season. Second, the sections quietly teach our long game without lecturing: the elementary section is pure wide exposure, the middle section is taste testing and habit, the early HS section is about finding the one or two things worth going deep on, and the upper HS section is where depth converts into output and credentials. A parent who reads the ladder top to bottom absorbs the philosophy by osmosis. Third, forwarding: the senior parent forwards the whole email to the friend with a fourth grader, and the email already contains that friend's section. Every send recruits.

Stage weighting per edition: upper HS gets the most space and ALL the deadlines, early HS gets the events, middle school gets two or three items, elementary gets one warm paragraph at the bottom, never more. We say this plainly in the design, not in the copy.

One craft rule per item: lead with the date and the word free when true, one action per item, official link only.
