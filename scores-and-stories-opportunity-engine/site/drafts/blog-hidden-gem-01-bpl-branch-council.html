Draft body (for Megan's edit, 150 words, facts only)

Brooklyn Public Library runs an internship that most families have never heard of. The Teen Takeovers Branch Council hires high school students, ages 16 to 19, to plan and host the after hours teen events at their local branches. Participants receive a stipend and community service hours. The application is short and admission is rolling.

The substance is event production: programming choices, promotion, and hosting a public event inside a major civic institution. For a student, that is a leadership role with verifiable output. For an application reader, a paid position at Brooklyn Public Library is a concrete line with a concrete reference behind it.

Details and the application are at bklynlibrary.org under Support, then Volunteer. Students who want a longer library thread can also look at the BKLYN Library Youth Council, the system's standing teen advisory body.
