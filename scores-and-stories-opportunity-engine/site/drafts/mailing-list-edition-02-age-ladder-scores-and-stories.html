The one idea this email teaches (top of send, three sentences)

Colleges read two kinds of evidence: Scores, the credentials and numbers, and Stories, the lived experience that gives an application a voice. The trick almost nobody tells you is that the best opportunities feed both at once, and most of them are free. Every item below is tagged so you can see exactly which one it feeds.

The quiet vetting note (internal, for the counselor reply guide, not the send)

NYU SPS Career Edge commuter deadline is Friday June 12. $2,579 per course for one week, open admission with an essay. When a family asks: the honest answer is that item 2 (the library pays your kid) or item 1 (the museum pays your kid) reads better to an admissions office than any one week course a parent paid for. Free beats paid is not a slogan, it is how selectivity works.
