## Category 1: Selective research programs (Scores and Stories, Rung 4)

**Academic Year Fellowship, The Jackson Laboratory (JAX). [Both]**
* Rolling applications until August 24, 2026; projects run fall through late spring, 180 hours minimum on site.
* Eligibility: high school juniors and seniors. Cost: unpaid. Location: Bar Harbor, Maine and Farmington, Connecticut (out of NYC area).
* Grade group: 11 and 12. Wagner angle: a genuine genetics research credential that is open right now, with two real barriers stated up front, unpaid and out of area.
* Link: https://www.jax.org/education-and-learning/high-school-students-and-undergraduates/academic-year-internships

**Simons Summer Research Program, Stony Brook University. [Both]**
* 2026 cycle is closed (was February 5). Next cycle opens in winter for summer 2027.
* Eligibility: 11th grade at time of applying, US citizen or permanent resident, 16 by program start, school nomination required, maximum two students per school. Location: Long Island, LIRR accessible (Ring 2).
* Grade group: current 10th graders (rising 11th). Wagner angle: the nomination cap means the school conversation has to start a year early; this is a 2027 planning item, not a June action.
* Link: https://www.stonybrook.edu/simons/

## Category 2: Science fairs and the research ladder (Scores, Rung 4)

**New York City Science and Engineering Fair (NYCSEF). [Both]**
* Annual citywide fair, the ISEF affiliate, roughly 700 students. School year entry begins in fall, fair in late winter. Free.
* Eligibility: grades 9 to 12 taking relevant courses. Location: all five boroughs, CUNY host campuses.
* Grade group: 9 to 12. Wagner angle: the destination for any school year research project, free, and the entry process is a fall task, not a spring scramble.
* Link: https://nycsef.org/

**New York State Science and Engineering Fair (NYSSEF). [Scores]**
* The state level fair and rules body that sits above the regional fairs.
* Link: https://www.nyssef.org/
* Wagner angle: the next rung for a strong NYCSEF project; confirm the current year's regional eligibility before advising.

## Category 3: Free, accessible, resume building science (Stories into Scores, Rung 2 to 3)

**Science Career Ladder, Explainer program, New York Hall of Science. [Both]**
* Paid, rolling, a documented multi year employment ladder for teens on the museum floor in Queens.
* Link: https://nysci.org (program profile: https://www.snow.day/learning-opportunities/35dfd584-2382-4eb0-8237-bee829b20847/science-career-ladder-scl-explainer)
* Grade group: 9 to 12. Wagner angle: the strongest free, low barrier science item for Queens families, and it pays.

**Biorocket Youth Research Internship, Genspace, Brooklyn. [Both]**
* $2,000 stipend, genetic engineering research. At least 16, NYC public or charter school within a 45 minute commute to Sunset Park. January deadline pattern.
* Link: https://www.genspace.org/biorocket
* Grade group: current 10th and 11th. Wagner angle: a paid lab research entry point that lives inside the city, applied for in January.

## Category 4: Finance and business competitions (Scores, Rung 4)

**Wharton Global High School Investment Competition. [Scores]**
* Free team competition, grades 9 to 12, teams of four to six with a teacher advisor. The 2025-26 cycle registered June 30 to September 12; the 2026-27 window is expected to open within days.
* Location: online competition, in person Global Finale at The Wharton School, Philadelphia.
* Grade group: 9 to 12. Wagner angle: a free named university credential; the action this month is lining up a teacher advisor before registration opens.
* Link: https://globalyouth.wharton.upenn.edu/competitions/investment-competition/

**High School Fed Challenge, Federal Reserve Bank of New York. [Scores]**
* Team economics research and writing competition, grades 9 to 12. Last cycle registered in fall, closed February 17; next cycle expected fall 2026.
* Link: https://www.newyorkfed.org/outreach-and-education/high-school/high-school-fed-challenge
* Grade group: 9 to 12. Wagner angle: a local, free finance and economics credential to pair with or substitute for the Wharton competition.

## Professional takeaways (for the team)
* Open right now: JAX (rolling to August 24) and NYCSEF (fall entry). Wharton 2026-27 registration opens within days; the teacher advisor is the gating step, so secure it now.
* Closed for this year, plan ahead: Simons (winter 2027, school nomination in 11th grade), Fed Challenge (fall 2026), Biorocket (January 2027).
* Geography and cost are the honest filters: NYCSEF, Science Career Ladder, Biorocket, and Fed Challenge are free and local; JAX is unpaid and out of state; Simons is Long Island and nomination capped.
* The free local competitions read at least as strongly to admissions as the out of area paid or unpaid placements, and they keep the family's time and money in reserve.
