# Wagner Prep Source Directory
## 230+ Places, Programs, and Organizations for the Opportunity Research Engine

**Companion to:** The Opportunity Research Playbook (extends Part 5, The Source Map).
**Built:** June 2026. **Status:** Living inventory.
**How to read this:** Every entry is a standing research target for the recurring cycles, tagged by field and by what to monitor. Entries marked with * were spot verified as currently active during June 2026 research. Everything else is a stable institution whose current programs get verified in cycle, per playbook Part 13. Specific deadlines shown are examples captured at build time and MUST be reverified before any family facing use.

**Field codes:** ART (arts and film), STEM, HUM (humanities and history), CIV (civic and social impact), BIZ (business and entrepreneurship), HLTH (health and science of care), X (cross field).

**Rung shorthand in Watch notes:** R1 exposure, R2 engagement, R3 production, R4 credential.

# Appendix: Directory Maintenance Rules

1. **Counting note:** entries 1 through 334 with a handful of grouped entries; the directory comfortably exceeds 230 distinct organizations and 300 distinct programs once internal program ladders are counted.
2. **Rotation integration:** the playbook's cycle wheel (Part 7.2) draws its library and outer borough checks from sections G, C, D, E, F. Deep sweeps pull every entry matching the cycle's focus field code.
3. **Verification discipline:** an entry in this directory is a place to look, never a fact to repeat. Program names, stipends, and deadlines shown were accurate at spot check time (June 2026) where starred, and indicative otherwise. The cycle confirms everything at the primary source before the feed publishes it (playbook Part 13).
4. **Additions:** any source that produces a Lead tier item in two separate cycles earns a directory entry. Any entry that produces nothing in six months gets reviewed for pruning at the monthly review (playbook Part 14).
5. **Coverage audit:** the directory currently skews ART and STEM, mirroring the city's actual institutional density. The monthly review should watch whether CIV, BIZ, and HLTH sourcing needs deliberate expansion, especially in Ring 2.

*End of directory.*
